CHRISTINE HOLT

Micro Fiction

Reflection:

How did it feel to redraft this piece?

I was surprised by the amount of time I struggled with redrafting this piece. It is so short, but I really needed to bog down and properly think about how I am going to redraft it so every word is used effectively. I was not struggling with keeping it within the character limit like I thought I would, but I did struggle with which words to use so everything still makes sense and so a story still emerges from this piece, even though it is so short.

I did, however, enjoy redrafting this one the most. I really enjoyed the micro-fiction task. It isn’t an easy task, but it is fun and is something I would like to do more often.

What do you like most about your piece, why?

I enjoy that it is punchy. I feel like there is feeling and tension in each sentence. It is short but it tells a story, and I feel as if it gives a good indication of what the world of the story looks like at that moment in time.

What part of your piece needs the most improvement, why?

I feel as if there might still be parts that are unnecessary or could have been stated differently. The part where the character crashes into the shack still feels weird. I did not know which word to use, or whether the sentence was even necessary, but it felt like it needed to be there, so I left it in.

Also, the second paragraph, I am still not sure if I should have stated that differently. I went through, like, seven different ways to write that sentence, and settled on ‘She has to run’, but I am still not sure whether it feels right.

Final Draft

Her heartbeat thumps in her ears. Pain shoots down her legs.

She has to run.

Sweat streaks down her dusty face. Her throat burns.

Run.

She crashes into a crumbling shack. Her breathing loud.

She looks up. UFOs everywhere.

The conspiracies were right.

Aliens do exist.
